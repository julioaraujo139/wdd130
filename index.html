Brazil

Paraná is a state in southern Brazil known for its rich agriculture, diverse climate, and strong economy. Its capital is Curitiba, a model city for urban planning and sustainability. Paraná is home to part of the Iguaçu Falls, one of the world’s largest and most spectacular waterfalls, located on the border with Argentina. The state has a mix of European, Indigenous, and African cultural influences and plays a key role in Brazil’s production of soy, corn, and poultry. It is an important region for both natural beauty and economic development in Brazil.
